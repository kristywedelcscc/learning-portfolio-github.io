### a. What emerging technology did you choose? How is AI being used to support this technology?

The emerging technology chosen is **autonomous vehicles (self-driving cars)**.  
Artificial Intelligence is used to analyze sensor data, detect objects, make driving decisions, and navigate roads without human input. AI systems process information from cameras, radar, and lidar to recognize pedestrians, traffic signs, vehicles, and road conditions in real time.

---

### b. What previous solutions were being used in place of AI? How has AI replaced or will soon replace these solutions?

Before AI-enabled systems, vehicles relied on **human drivers and rule-based automation** such as cruise control, parking sensors, and lane-assist features. These systems used predefined programming rather than learning from data. AI allows vehicles to **learn from large datasets and improve decision-making**, enabling more advanced capabilities like adaptive driving, automatic braking, and full self-driving navigation.

---

### c. Briefly describe the type of AI being used for this technology

Autonomous vehicles use several types of AI:

- **Computer Vision** – to identify objects such as pedestrians, road signs, and other vehicles.
- **Machine Learning / Deep Learning** – to learn driving patterns and improve decision-making from large datasets.
- **Sensor Fusion Algorithms** – to combine data from cameras, radar, and lidar for accurate environmental understanding.

---

### d. How does the use of AI and this technology affect everyday life?

AI-powered autonomous vehicles could improve everyday life by **increasing road safety, reducing traffic accidents, and improving transportation efficiency**. They may also provide mobility to people who cannot drive, such as the elderly or individuals with disabilities, and could reduce traffic congestion through optimized driving patterns.

---

### e. Are there any concerns with using AI for this application?

Yes, there are several concerns:

- **Safety and reliability** – AI systems must perform correctly in unpredictable situations.
- **Ethical decision-making** – how vehicles should respond in unavoidable accident scenarios.
- **Cybersecurity risks** – connected vehicles could be vulnerable to hacking.
- **Legal and regulatory challenges** – determining liability when accidents occur.
- **Job displacement** – impacts on professions such as taxi and truck drivers.
